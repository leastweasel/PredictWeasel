Scoring Playing PredictWeasel Playing the game

The aim of the game is to score as many points as you can by predicting the outcome of various matches. The more accurate your prediction, the more points it will score. How you define accurate is, of course, rather subjective and can vary from league to league, but there is a default formula.

Campetitions

In PredictWeasel you're predicting the results of matches that form part of a real-world competition. The FIFA World Cup, for example. An administrator will set up a competition and enter its fixtures into the system. It's now possible to create leagues based on that competition.

Leagues

A league exists only within PredictWeasel. Any player can create a league by giving it a name, selecting the competition it is to be based on, and offering one, two or three prizes (see below). Players subscribe to a league, normally as a result of receiving an invitation from the player who created the league (who is also allowed to play in it). Players will soon be able to play in more than one league simultaneously but only one subscription is possible at the moment.

Prizes

Players in a league compete to score more points than each other in up to three prizes. Each prize has its own way of calculating how many points your predictions are worth, or even whether a fixture should be included. There are currently three prize categories in PredictWeasel:

Overall
Adds up the points scored by all fixtures in the competition
Knock-out
Adds up the points scored by all knock-out fixtures in the competition. This prize is normally included so that players who've had a shocker in the group stages still have something to play for.
Spot-on
You score one point for each prediction where you get the score exactly right.

You can always see how you're doing in your league by checking the abbreviated standings shown in the league's home page, or the full standings, which are linked to from the abbreviated ones.

Entering predictions

You can enter predictions in two places: from the league's home page, which shows the next few fixtures to be played, or the full list of fixtures. It's the same procedure in both places. There's an input field for each fixture. Type in your prediction, which should be in the form of a number, a space or a dash. then another number.

Your prediction will be submitted as soon as you tab out of (or otherwise leave) the input field. If you get the format wrong you'll get an error message, and the prediction will revert to your previously saved version, or blank if this is a new prediction. If it's OK you'll be told it has been saved.
