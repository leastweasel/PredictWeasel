Scoring Predictions How many points are your predictions worth?

It's not possible to give a definitive guide on how many points a particular prediction will score as the scoring method can potentially vary from league to league. The following is a description of the default scoring method, The Livermore Scoring Model, that has been used in all past versions of PredictWeasel and is, in fact, the only one available at the moment.

The Livermore Scoring Model

For each fixture, the first named team is the 'home' team, the other team is the 'away' team.

  • Correctly guessing the result; home win, away win or draw, scores 7 points
  • You gain extra points for correctly guessing how many goals either side scores, 1 point per goal
  • You also gain points for correctly guessing the difference in the scores of the two teams. Again, score 1 point per goal
  • However, you lose points for incorrectly guessing a team's score; 1 point for each goal by which you are wrong.

As an example, suppose that a friendly, Arsenal v Lincoln City, ends in an improbable 1-3 win for the mighty Imps.

Mick played the percentages and went for a comfortable 2-0 win for the former champions. Apart from the embarrassment he also had to suffer scoring -4 point for his prediction: 0 points for getting the result wrong, -1 for Arsenal's goals, and -3 for Lincoln's goals.

Being a Lincoln fan, Andrew ignored common sense and went for a 1-2 win. He scored 7 for the result and 1 for Arsenal's goal but lost a point for being one out with Lincoln's goals. Total score, 7 points.

Extra time and penalties

If there were a Frequently Asked Questions page on this site it would have one question on it and that would ask how we score matches that go into extra time.

The answer is that it's the score at the end of normal play that counts. So, if there's extra time, it's the score at the end of extra time that counts. If not, it's the score at the end of 90 minutes. The score of a penalty shoot-out is never used.
